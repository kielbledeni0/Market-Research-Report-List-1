<p><p>The retinal detachment diagnostic market encompasses technologies and services aimed at identifying three primary types of retinal detachments: rhegmatogenous, exudative, and tractional. Rhegmatogenous detachment, caused by retinal tears, is the most common type; diagnostic tools focus on imaging to detect breaks. Exudative detachment results from fluid accumulation under the retina; diagnostics involve assessing underlying conditions like tumors. Tractional detachment occurs due to fibrous tissue pulling on the retina, requiring specialized imaging techniques for accurate diagnosis and management.</p></p>
