<p><p>Whole Genome Sequencing (WGS) and Exome Sequencing (ES) are advanced genomic techniques used to analyze an organism's genetic material. WGS involves sequencing the entire genome, providing comprehensive insights into both coding and non-coding regions, thereby revealing mutations, structural variants, and regulatory elements. In contrast, exome sequencing targets only the exons, or coding regions of genes, which comprise about 1-2% of the genome but contain most known disease-related variants. </p><p>The Whole Genome and Exome Sequencing Market is witnessing significant growth, driven by increasing applications in personalized medicine, drug development, and genetic research. Additionally, technological advancements, such as next-generation sequencing (NGS), have made these processes more efficient and cost-effective. Rising demand for detailed genetic information and the growing prevalence of genetic disorders are further propelling market expansion. The market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 20.00% during the forecast period, reflecting a strong interest from both healthcare providers and researchers.
