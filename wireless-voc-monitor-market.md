<p><p>The wireless VOC (volatile organic compounds) monitor market encompasses various types of technologies, primarily focusing on Photoionization Detectors (PID) and Metal-Oxide Semiconductors (MOS). PID monitors utilize ultraviolet light to ionize gas molecules, enabling sensitive detection of a wide range of VOCs. In contrast, Metal-Oxide Semiconductor sensors operate by altering electrical resistance in response to gas exposure, making them effective for various applications. Both technologies enhance real-time air quality monitoring, contributing to environmental safety and worker health management.</p></p>
